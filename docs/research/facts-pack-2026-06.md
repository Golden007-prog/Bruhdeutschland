## 1. Application Process & Timeline

**End-to-end steps (non-EU Master's applicant)** — source: [DAAD Application process](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/requirements/application-process/)
1. Check admission restrictions (NC / *numerus clausus*) for the program.
2. Determine the **application pathway**: Stiftung für Hochschulzulassung, **uni-assist**, or **directly to the university** — depends on country of origin, your entrance qualification, and subject.
3. Gather documents (certified copies of degree/transcripts, language certificate, ID, APS where applicable).
4. Submit via the correct channel (often before a separate **VPD** deadline — see below).
5. Await decision (acceptance/*Zulassung* or rejection).

**Intake cycles & typical deadlines** — source: [DAAD](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/requirements/application-process/), [uni-assist deadlines](https://www.uni-assist.de/en/how-to-apply/plan-your-application/deadlines-processing-time/)

| Cycle | Starts | Typical deadline | Decisions | Volatility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wintersemester (WS)** | ~Oct | **~15 July** (apps open ~early May) | Aug–Sep | HIGH (varies by uni/program) |
| **Sommersemester (SS)** | ~Apr | **~15 January** (apps open ~early Dec) | Feb–Mar | HIGH |

- ⚠️ **Non-EU applicants often face EARLIER deadlines** than EU applicants (real examples: WS 31 May, 15 Apr, or 15 June; SS 15 Oct, 15 Dec). Master's programs frequently set earlier-than-standard deadlines. **Always verify per program.**

**uni-assist — when used vs. direct** — source: [uni-assist VPD](https://www.uni-assist.de/en/how-to-apply/plan-your-application/vpd/)
- uni-assist is used **when the target university is a member / requires it**. Otherwise you apply **directly**. It is a *per-university* choice — not universal.

**VPD (Vorprüfungsdokumentation)** — source: [uni-assist VPD](https://www.uni-assist.de/en/how-to-apply/plan-your-application/vpd/)
- A certificate from uni-assist stating which entrance qualification your certificates confer and your grade converted to the German scale. Submit it **directly to the university**.
- **Needed** when a (non-uni-assist) university requires a VPD instead of a full uni-assist application.
- **Processing:** usually **4–6 weeks**. **Validity:** **1 year**. Must reach the university **before its deadline**.

## 2. APS Certificate (Akademische Prüfstelle)

- **What it is:** Joint institution of the **German Embassy + DAAD** that verifies authenticity of academic certificates. Source: [aps-germany.org](https://aps-germany.org/en/), [uni-assist glossary: APS](https://www.uni-assist.de/en/tools/glossary-of-terms/description/term/akademische-pruefstelle-aps/)
- **Countries requiring it (key ones):** **India, China, Vietnam.** "Your application cannot be processed without this certificate."
- **India — mandatory date (CONFIRMED):** APS New Delhi opened **1 October 2022**; from **1 November 2022** the APS certificate became a **mandatory part of the student-visa application.** Source: [German Mission India newsletter (04 Oct 2022)](https://india.diplo.de/in-en/ueber-uns/mumbai/visa-newsletter-04oct2022/2566330)

| Value | Detail | Source | Volatility / Year |
|---|---|---|---|
| Countries | India, China, Vietnam | aps-germany.org; uni-assist | LOW |
| India mandatory since | 1 Nov 2022 | diplo.de India newsletter | LOW (historical) |

## 3. GPA & Credits

**Modified Bavarian Formula:** `x = 1 + 3 × (Nmax − Nd) / (Nmax − Nmin)`
- `Nmax` = best grade; `Nmin` = min pass; `Nd` = grade to convert; `x` = German equivalent.
- **German scale:** **1.0 = best**, **4.0 = lowest pass**, **5.0 = fail.**
- ⚠️ The literal formula was **not** confirmed on a single official DAAD/uni-assist page; canonical source is Anabin/KMK "Modifizierte Bayerische Formel." Per CLAUDE.md rule #4, conversion runs in **tested deterministic code**, never the LLM.

**ECTS basics** — source: [EU – ECTS](https://education.ec.europa.eu/education-levels/higher-education/inclusive-and-connected-higher-education/european-credit-transfer-and-accumulation-system)

| ECTS per year | **60** | LOW |

## 4. Language & Test Requirements

> **All thresholds are PROGRAM-SPECIFIC.** Store as defaults/ranges. Source: [DAAD Requirements](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/requirements/overview/)

**English-taught:** IELTS Academic **6.5** typical (5.5–7.0 range); TOEFL iBT **~85–90** (79–100). English-taught programs generally exempt from German proof.

**German-taught:** TestDaF **TDN 4 in all sections**; DSH **DSH-2**; Goethe **C1/C2**; telc **C1 Hochschule**; baseline ≥ **B2 CEFR**.

## 5. Exam Formats (for mock-exam content)

> **Format-churn flags:** TOEFL iBT redesigned **Jan 2026** (new 1–6 scale); GRE short format since **Sep 2023**; GMAT **Focus Edition** (205–805).

### IELTS Academic — [format](https://ielts.org/organisations/ielts-for-organisations/test-types/ielts-academic-test/academic-test-format-in-detail)
Total 2h45m. **Band 0–9.** Listening ~30m/40Q · Reading 60m/40Q · Writing 60m (Task 1+2) · Speaking 11–14m.
Example types: T/F/Not Given; Matching headings; Summary completion; Task 1 graph description; Speaking cue-card.

### TOEFL iBT — [content](https://www.ets.org/toefl/test-takers/ibt/about/content.html)
**HIGH volatility (post-Jan-2026).** ~2h. New scale **1–6 per section + overall** (0–120 also reported during transition). Reading 30m/50 · Listening 29m/47 · Speaking 8m/11 · Writing 23m/12.
Example types: "Write for an Academic Discussion"; academic-passage MCQ; "Take an Interview."

### TestDaF (digital) — [structure](https://www.testdaf.de/de/teilnehmende/der-digitale-testdaf/aufbau-des-digitalen-testdaf/)
Scoring **TDN 3/4/5** per section (~CEFR B2–C1). Lesen 34Q/~55m · Hören 30Q/~40m · Schreiben 2 tasks/~60m · Sprechen 7 tasks/~35m.

### Goethe-Zertifikat (CEFR A1–C2) — [B2](https://www.goethe.de/ins/us/en/spr/prf/gzb2.cfm)
4 modules: Lesen/Hören/Schreiben/Sprechen. Pass mark **60%** (verify per level). B2: Lesen 65m · Hören 40m · Schreiben 75m · Sprechen 15m.

### GRE General — [structure](https://www.ets.org/gre/test-takers/general-test/prepare/test-structure.html)
~1h58m, 5 sections. **Verbal 130–170; Quant 130–170; Writing 0–6.** Example types: Text Completion; Sentence Equivalence; Quantitative Comparison; Numeric Entry.

### GMAT Focus Edition — [structure](https://www.mba.com/exams/gmat-exam/about/exam-structure)
2h15m, 64Q. **Total 205–805**; each section 60–90. Quant 21Q/45m · Verbal 23Q/45m · Data Insights 20Q/45m.
Example types: Problem Solving; Critical Reasoning; Data Sufficiency; Two-Part Analysis.

## 6. Finance

| Value | Detail | Volatility / Year |
|---|---|---|
| Required amount | **€11,904 / year (€992 / month)** | **HIGH** / since 1 Jan 2025, current 2026 |
| Alternatives | parents' income · Verpflichtungserklärung (§§66–68 AufenthG) · bank guarantee · scholarship | LOW |
| Providers (examples) | **Fintiba, Expatrio, Coracle / Deutsche Bank** | not officially endorsed — mark UNVERIFIED |

**Tuition / Semesterbeitrag** — source: [DAAD Costs](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/living-in-germany/finances/)

**Health insurance** — source: [TK student contributions](https://www.tk.de/en/service-faqs/join-tk/contribution-students-2038022), [make-it-in-germany](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/living-in-germany/money-insurance/health-insurance)

**Deutschlandticket / Semesterticket** — source: [Deutschlandticket 2026 price](https://wissen.deutschlandticket.de/wie-viel-kostet-das-deutschland-ticket-ab-dem-1.-januar-2026-0)
- Deutschlandticket **€63 / month** (raised from €58, since 1 Jan 2026). Deutschland-Semesterticket = discounted per-uni share.

**Working-hour limits (non-EU)** — source: [Make-it-in-Germany Study and work](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/study-vocational-training/studies-in-germany/work), [DAAD Side jobs](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/work-career/side-jobs/)
- **140 full / 280 half days per year** (raised from 120/240); half-day = up to 4 hrs; ~20 hrs/week in lecture period. HiWi/student-assistant jobs unrestricted.

## 7. Scholarships

**DAAD** — Master's stipend **€992/month** (2026; replaces stale €934), +€460/yr study allowance, +insurance; 10–24 months. Eligibility: degree ≤6 yrs old, not resident in Germany >15 months. DB: funding-guide.de. Source: [DAAD scholarships](https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/scholarships/daad-scholarships/)

**Deutschlandstipendium** — **€300/month** (€150 sponsor + €150 federal); all nationalities; merit-based; university-run; ≥2 semesters. Source: [deutschlandstipendium.de](https://www.deutschlandstipendium.de/)

**Erasmus+** — EU Commission via National Agencies/EACEA. Non-EU routes: International Credit Mobility (KA171), Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters (EMJM, full scholarships). Apply through a participating HEI. Source: [Erasmus+](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/)

## 8. Visa & Relocation (non-EU)

**German National (Student) Visa** — source: [Auswärtiges Amt FAQ](https://www.auswaertiges-amt.de/en/visa-service/buergerservice/faq/08-studentenvisum-606690)

| Item | Detail | Volatility / Year |
|---|---|---|
| Type | **National visa "D"** (>90 days); convert to residence permit at the Ausländerbehörde | LOW |
| Where | German embassy/consulate (sometimes VFS) | LOW |
| Processing | **Several weeks – months** | **HIGH** / 2026 |
| Fee | **€75** | **HIGH** / 2026 |

**Required documents:** passport · national-visa form + declaration · **admission letter** · **proof of financing (blocked account)** · **health insurance** · academic certificates · language proof · **APS (India/China/Vietnam)** · biometric photo. Exact list is mission-specific.

**Anmeldung (address registration)** — governed by Bundesmeldegesetz. Register at the **Bürgeramt within 14 days** of moving in; need passport, **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung** (landlord confirmation), and the form. Source: [Make-it-in-Germany](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/living-in-germany/housing-mobility/housing-registration)
